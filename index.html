Tic-Tac-Toe

How to play?

Tic-Tac-Toe is a classic two-player game played on a 3x3 grid. The goal is to be the first player to get three of your symbols in a row.

1. Set Up the Board

Draw a grid with two horizontal lines and two vertical lines, creating nine squares.

  1. Player 1 is usually X
  2. Player 2 is usually O

2. Take Turns

Decide who goes first. Players take turns placing their mark (X or O) in an empty square.

Tip: Once a mark is placed, it cannot be changed or moved!

3. The Objective

The goal is to align three of your marks in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal row.

Winning Patterns Example
Horizontal Three in a row across (top, middle, or bottom).
Vertical Three in a row down (left, center, or right).
Diagonal Three in a row slanting from corner to corner.

Winning or Drawing

  1. Winner: The first person to get three in a row wins the game.
  2. Draw (Tie): If all nine squares are filled and no one has three in a row, the game is a "Draw" (often called a "Catâ€™s Game").
